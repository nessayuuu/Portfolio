Design Project -

Project Overview
Wanderers is an app that aims to bring together three aspects of affordable traveling: locations, lodging, and transportation. Perfect for the worldwide adventurer and local area enthusiasts. Come discover the local favorites so you can become a local too.
View Project

Project Type

Solo

Role

UX/UI Designer.

Duration

Two Weeks

Scope

Design a travel application.

Problem Statement.

Users are over-saturated with the volume of side-by-side lodging comparison app
and sites user reviews. Most, if not all, lack local users and reviewers for an in-depth experience.

User Personas.

To gain a better understanding of travelers who are looking for affordable travel, I created one user persona: Sandra Smith.

Sandra Smith -

is a single 25 year-old financial analyst. Her interests include finding new food to eat, traveling to find new food and her two dogs. To complement Sandra’s hobbies, her needs include: exploring the world on a budget, and balancing her lifestyle with her expenses. Some of her frustrations include needing to switch between applications and websites to find the best deals, and hours of operations for restaurants. A potential solution is an application that condenses prices for lodging, food, and hours of operation at new locations.

The Process.

Define.

My first step was to define Wanderer’s goals. To define the goals, I created a survey, and conducted user interviews with the younger general public whose age ranged from 22-28 years old. From there I was able to define three goals: narrowing down local locations, providing the most affordable lodging and transportation options.

Prototype.

The second step was to develop mobile wireframes for user testing. I took responses from both the user surveys and user interviews to locate the most common pain points and design a solution.

User Test Outcomes.

I took the initial wireframes and asked random individuals to test it and provide feedback. Initial reactions were met with confusion as the users were not provided with a clear task. Instead, they had to be walked through to complete the task. Taking a step back, I had to redefine what is a logical workflow for users, not just me as an individual. That took me back to the drawing board where I redesigned the application based on the feedback received from users.

Despite the initial confusion, users immensely enjoyed the idea of collecting three aspects of traveling and placing it into one app to save time, energy and money.

Visual Design.

In preparation for my final design, I created a color style guide that would have sky and earthy tones to match a sunrise over the horizon. Sprinkled with pastel tones to create a sense of peace and wonder.

Iterations.

The third step was user testing. Taking feedback from the users and implementing it into my re-design process was crucial for the next part of my process: the final design.

Redesign.

My redesign after implementing user feedback for mobile.

Takeaway.

My lesson in this project how crucial user research is. User Research was the largest influencer
in iterating my design to better complement the users and not me as the designer. I realized my initial designs
were centered around how I saw the flow of actions but not necessarily how everyone else would see it.
Learning to let go of my earlier designs and taking the leap into creating a whole new experience
for the users was eye-opening in thinking outside the box, outside my world and into everyone else's.

Future Opportunities

With the growth of Wanderers as an application, I look to one day integrate
collaborations with different lodging companies and transportation companies.
To encourage young travelers that seeing the world isn’t impossible and won’t break the bank.
