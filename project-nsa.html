Design Project -

Project Overview
The National Security Agency (NSA) is responsible for collection, counterintelligence, and data processing both domestic and abroad. The NSA is also in charge of protecting the United States communication networks and information systems.
View Project

Project Type

Solo

Role

UX/UI Designer

Duration

Two Weeks

Scope

Fill a contact form and view leadership history.

Problem Statement.

Government websites are a wealth of resources but
lack the organization necessary to make it understandable for the general public.

User Personas.

To gain a better understanding of individuals who would utilize a government site,
I created Charlotte Buckley and Martha Gonzalez.

Charlotte Buckley -

is a single 27 year-old graduate student. As she’s preparing to graduate from her masters in Criminology and Information Systems she’s looking to build her career. Her interests include watching criminal shows, reading news articles and growing her succulent collection. One of her largest needs is to find a job and discover more about the company that she’s applying for. The biggest obstacle she is facing is finding information on both job postings and the company. Due to the disorganization, she finds herself going onto other sites for the same information. By offering a site with easily accessible information, Charlotte is more willing to continue using the site.

Martha Gonzalez -

is a married 34 year-old news reporter with a Masters in journalism. Her interests include analyzing
news articles, history and documentaries. As news is always changing, she needs to be able to
view information in an accessible and efficient fashion. In addition, she needs to get the facts right
as to avoid leading her viewers astray. That’s not always the case and one of Martha’s largest frustrations.
By presenting the information in an efficient format, Martha can get the facts straight from the start.

The Process.

Define.

Our first step was to define the NSA’s website goals. To define the goals, I conducted a card sorting activity and red-lining as to way to condense information. From there I was able to define four goals: about the NSA, latest updates from the NSA, join the NSA and NSA resources. This makes up the entirety of the navigation.

Prototype.

The second step was to develop website wireframes for user testing. I created a basic outline of how the site would work and asked users to test it for viewing current leaders of the NSA and filling out a form for more information.

User Test Outcomes.

I took the initial wireframes then asked random individuals to test the website and mobile version to provide feedback. Initial reactions were met with confusion as users focus was shifted to job postings. It was the largest section of the page and users weren’t able to discern how to find information and fill out a form. Others found the left column of text to be repetitive and information too condensed so the effect was overwhelming.

The first step was to remove the left column of text. The next step was to not place emphasis on job postings for the NSA. By spacing out the information into horizontal sections on the page, I was able to achieve a cleaner and more easily digestible organization of information.

Visual Design.

In preparation for my final design, I created a color style guide that would have business-like tones to emulate a formal company. Sprinkled with bright icons to keep the effect light but professional.

Iterations.

The following step was iterations. Taking feedback from the users and implementing it into our design was crucial for the next part of our design process: the final design.

Redesign.

My redesign after implementing user feedback for both website and mobile.

Takeaway.

My lesson in this project how crucial accurate and efficient information is.
Too much information and individuals will quickly shift their attention elsewhere.
Too little information and individuals will still shift their attention elsewhere to obtain more information.
Potentially shifting their attention to a site that may not present information
as accurately as the original site. Locating that middle ground of information
is key in maintaining user retention and encouraging more.

Future Opportunities

By fully reorganizing and redesigning the site, the NSA can create transparency with the public.
Transparency will deter the spread of misinformation and encourage users like Charlotte and Martha to continue using the site.
